The Business of Culture

Business lessons through film, literature, and culture

May 20, 2017

I am currently obsessed with the scientific method. In my independent studies of business literature within the past two months, the majority of the 18 books I have read cite the scientific method or an equivalent learning cycle as crucial to business success. Drucker-inspired time logs of my hourly schedule provide ample ground for hypothesizing and earlier experimentation which together have brought me to my current hypothesis: business lessons can be made more interesting by leveraging film, literature, and culture.

To test my hypothesis, I am embarking on a new project. Every week I will be examining culture through the lens of different business frameworks. Would Drucker consider Don Draper an effective executive? Does Dumbledore exibit Level 5 leadership? Is Luffy's crew structure a disruptive innovation relative to other crews?

I'll publish my first experiment next Friday, May 26. Until then, cheers!
