What makes an effective executive? What traits distinguish them from the average knowledge worker? Who are exemplary examples of effectiveness? The Effectiveness Framework, based on Peter Drucker's book "The Effective Executive," is a framework with the potential to answer those questions. In the The Business of Culture series' first entry, we use the framework to evaluate and compare Scott Pilgrim from "Scott Pilgrim's Precious Little Life," and Kōjirō Shindō from "Kado: The Right Answer."
